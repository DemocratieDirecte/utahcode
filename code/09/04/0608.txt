9-4-608.   Misconduct of commissioners -- Removal.

(1)  For inefficiency, neglect of duty, or misconduct in office, a commissioner of an authority may be removed by the mayor or, in the case of an authority for a county, by the body that appointed the commissioner.

(2)  A commissioner may be removed only after a hearing and after he has been given a copy of the charges at least 10 days prior to the hearing and had an opportunity to be heard in person or by counsel.

(3)  If a commissioner is removed, a record of the proceedings, together with the charges and findings, shall be filed in the office of the clerk.
