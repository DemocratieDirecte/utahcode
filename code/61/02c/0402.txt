61-2c-402 (Superseded 01/01/10).   Disciplinary action.
Subject to the requirements of Section 61-2c-402.1, if a person required to be
licensed under this chapter violates this chapter, or an education provider
required to be certified under this chapter violates this chapter, the
commission, with the concurrence of the director, may:
(1)  impose an educational requirement;
(2)  impose a civil penalty against the individual or entity in an amount not
to exceed the greater of:
(a)  $2,500 for each violation; or
(b)  the amount equal to any gain or economic benefit derived from each
violation;
(3)  deny an application for an original license;
(4)  do any of the following to a license under this chapter:
(a)  suspend;
(b)  revoke;
(c)  place on probation;
(d)  deny renewal;
(e)  deny reinstatement; or
(f)  in the case of a denial or revocation of a license, set a waiting period
for a person to apply for a license under this chapter;
(5)  issue a cease and desist order;
(6)  require the reimbursement of the division of costs incurred by the
division related to the recovery, storage, or destruction of a record that the
person disposes of in a manner that violates this chapter or a rule made under
this chapter;
(7)  modify a sanction described in Subsections (1) through (6) if the
commission finds that the person complies with court ordered restitution; or
(8)  do any combination of Subsections (1) through (7).
