78B-8-302.   Process servers.
(1)  Complaints, summonses, and subpoenas may be served by any person 18 years
of age or older at the time of service, and who is not a party to the action
or a party's attorney.
(2)  The following persons may serve all process issued by the courts of this
state:
(a)  a peace officer employed by any political subdivision of the state acting
within the scope and jurisdiction of his employment;
(b)  a sheriff or appointed deputy sheriff employed by any county of the
state;
(c)  a constable, or the constable's deputy, serving in compliance with
applicable law; and
(d)  an investigator employed by the state and authorized by law to serve
civil process.
(3)  Private investigators licensed in accordance with Title 53, Chapter 9,
Private Investigator Regulation Act, may only serve the following forms of
process:
(a)  petitions;
(b)  complaints;
(c)  summonses;
(d)  supplemental orders;
(e)  orders to show cause;
(f)  notices;
(g)  small claims affidavits;
(h)  small claims orders;
(i)  writs of garnishment;
(j)  garnishee orders; and
(k)  subpoenas duces tecum.
(4)  Other persons may serve process as prescribed by Subsection (1).
(5)  A person serving process shall legibly document the date and time of
service and his name and address on the return of service.
