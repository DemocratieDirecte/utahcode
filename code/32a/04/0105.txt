32A-4-105 (Repealed 07/01/11).   Bond.

(1)  Each restaurant liquor licensee shall post a cash or corporate surety bond in the penal sum of $10,000 payable to the department, which the licensee has procured and must maintain for so long as the licensee continues to operate as a restaurant liquor licensee.

(2)  The bond shall be in a form approved by the attorney general, conditioned upon the licensee's faithful compliance with this title and the rules of the commission.

(3) (a)  If the $10,000 surety bond is canceled due to the licensee's negligence, a $300 reinstatement fee may be assessed.

(b)  No part of any cash or corporate bond so posted may be withdrawn:

(i)  during the period the license is in effect; or

(ii)  while revocation proceedings are pending against the licensee.

(c)  A bond filed by a licensee may be forfeited if the license is finally revoked.
