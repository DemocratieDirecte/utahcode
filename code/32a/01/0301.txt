32A-1-301 (Repealed 07/01/11).   Unlawful transfer or use of proof of age --
False information.
(1) (a)  It is unlawful for a person to transfer that person's proof of age to
another person to aid that person:
(i)  in procuring an alcoholic beverage or product;
(ii)  to gain admittance to a place where an alcoholic beverage or product is
sold or consumed; or
(iii)  to obtain employment that under this title may not be obtained by a
minor.
(b)  A person who permits that person's proof of age to be used by another for
a purpose stated in Subsection (1)(a) is guilty of a class B misdemeanor.
(2) (a)  It is unlawful for a person to use a proof of age containing false
information with the intent to:
(i)  procure an alcoholic beverage or product;
(ii)  gain admittance to a place where an alcoholic beverage or product is
sold or consumed; or
(iii)  obtain employment that under this title may not be obtained by a minor.
(b)  Except as provided in Section 32A-12-223, a person who violates this
Subsection (2) is guilty of a class A misdemeanor.
